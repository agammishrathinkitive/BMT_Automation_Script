Information Collected

Registration Information: When you create a BeanLogin account, you will be required to provide an email address that will be used as your login/username. In addition, when you create a BeanLogin account, we ask you to select a “Password”. That Password is the basis for the encryption key used in protecting your Encrypted Sensitive Information (see below). This Password is not known to A&I SOLUTIONS, INC.and is not stored in any digital form unless you explicitly ask to do so. When you enable specific services, we may collect additional information that you knowingly provide.

Billing information: When you make a purchase, we will collect all information necessary to complete the transaction, including your name and billing address. Other billing information necessary to complete the transaction, like your credit card information, will also be collected.

Encrypted Sensitive Information: In order to use the password vault and secure digital wallet, you will enter certain information into the App, that may include highly sensitive information like identity information, credit card information, app-specific passwords, receipts for online transactions and secure notes (“Encrypted Sensitive Information”). Each customer account within BeanLogin has a unique account key. This unique account key along with the user’s password is used to encrypt “The Encrypted Sensitive Information”. The unique account key is further encrypted by a master key that is securely stored separately from the account key. All of this encryption is performed on the BeanLogin server side using AES-256 symmetric encryption algorithm and none of it is viewable by A&I SOLUTIONS, INC. in unencrypted form.

Cookies are small text files that are stored on a user's computer by a Web server based on the permissions set in a user's browser software. A cookie itself is not designed or intended to read any information from a user's computer (other than the contents of the cookie); rather, it is an identifier used by the Web site that originally placed it on your hard drive. We may use both session Cookies (which expire once you close your web browser) and persistent Cookies (which stay on your computer until you delete them) to provide you with a more personal and interactive experience on our Site. This information is collected to make the Site more beneficial to you and to tailor the experience with us to meet your interests and needs.

You may adjust your Web browsers to either alert you of the use of cookies or refuse to accept cookies. Be aware that if you elect not to accept cookies, the Site may not function properly or permit access.
